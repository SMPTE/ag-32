This Administrative Guideline provides guidelines on how Artificial Intelligence (AI) may be used in the development of SMPTE standards. It is intended to help Standards Community (SC) Members understand how AI may be used to assist in the development of standards, and to provide guidance on how to use AI in a way that is consistent with the principles of standardization.

Purpose

This Administrative Guideline establishes requirements for the responsible use of Artificial Intelligence (AI). Its purpose is to:

  • Enable responsible, effective use of AI tools;
  • Protect SMPTE's intellectual property, confidentiality, privacy, and reputation; and
  • Ensure compliance with copyright, data protection, and other applicable laws.

Scope

This policy applies to:

  1. Covered Individuals: All SC Members and their representatives, and all other individuals participating in SC activities.
  2. Covered AI Tools: Generative AI systems and services that create, modify, or synthesize content, including:
    • Large language models (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini)
    • AI coding assistants (e.g., GitHub Copilot, CodeWhisperer)
    • AI-powered search and research tools that synthesize information
    • AI meeting assistants that transcribe, summarize, or generate content
    • AI features embedded in collaborative platforms and meeting software
    • Image, video, and audio generation tools used to create SC materials
  3. Responsibility. Individuals covered by this policy are responsible for ensuring that any tools they use in connection with content they contribute are permissible under, and in compliance with, this policy.

Classification of SC Materials

SC materials are classified as follows:

  1. Restricted Materials: Include, but are not limited to:
    • Draft standards, technical specifications, and normative documents prior to public review
    • Confidential member disclosures, submissions and contributions
    • Meeting materials, notes and minutes prior to public disclosure
    • Personal information and privacy-protected data
    • Internal working drafts of guides, white papers, and best practices
  2. Public Materials: Include, but are not limited to:
    • Published standards and specifications
    • Final versions of guides, white papers, and best practices
    • Public meeting materials and approved minutes
    • Content published on SMPTE's website or publicly available repositories

Mandatory Requirements

Disclosure Requirements

When contributing any content to SC that was created with or substantially assisted by AI tools, SC Members shall disclose:

  • That AI tools were used
  • The specific AI tool(s) used (name, and version if available)
  • The nature and extent of AI assistance (e.g., "drafted initial text," "generated code examples," "summarized research")
  1. Method of Disclosure
    • For contributions to any Engineering Document, or Other Published Document, as defined in : Include disclosure in Foreword section as defined in
    • For written contributions: Include disclosure in cover email, commit message, or document metadata
    • For meeting contributions: State at the time of contribution or in follow-up communication
    • For code contributions: Include disclosure in commit messages and/or comments

Training Data Restrictions

  • Restricted materials shall not be input into any AI tool that uses inputs for model training or improvement
  • Public materials may be used with training-enabled tools
  • SC Members shall confirm that any AI tool they use complies with these restrictions through review of applicable license terms, privacy policies, and tool settings

Accuracy and Verification Requirements

Before submitting any content to SC that was generated by or with the assistance of an AI tool, SC Members shall:

  • Review and verify its accuracy, completeness, and appropriateness
  • Ensure that any factual claims, data, or referenced materials are properly sourced and verified
  • Evaluate the content for potential bias, errors, or inappropriate material

AI Input Materials

  • SC Members shall only input materials into an AI where they have the legal right to use such materials for that purpose
  • Third-party copyrighted content shall not be input into AI tools where doing so would exceed fair use or applicable license terms

Code and Technical Contributions

  • AI tools may be used to assist with code drafting or analysis, but all code contributions shall comply with SMPTE's open source and/or licensing policies
  • Contributors are responsible for ensuring AI-assisted code does not introduce incompatible licenses, security vulnerabilities, or undisclosed dependencies
  • Automated code generation shall not bypass established review, testing, or contribution processes

SC Tools and Platforms

Meeting Platforms and Tools

Before procuring or enabling AI features in meeting platforms (Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet, etc.), SMPTE and/or SC Member shall:

  • Evaluate data retention and training policies
  • Assess privacy and security protections
  • Disable AI features that use meeting content for training unless contractually prohibited
  • Configure retention settings consistent with SMPTE's record retention policy
  • Inform meeting participants when AI tools are being used for transcription, summarization, or other purposes
  • Where required by law or policy, consent of all participants shall be obtained before recording or transcription

Collaborative Development Platforms

For platforms used for standards development (GitHub, GitLab, etc.), SMPTE shall:

  • Review and configure AI-assisted coding features in a manner consistent with this policy
  • Provide guidance to SC Members on configuring personal AI tools when contributing via these platforms

Appropriate and Inappropriate Uses

Appropriate Uses

AI tools may be used for the following purposes:

  • Generating initial drafts or outlines for review and substantial modification
  • Summarizing lengthy technical documents for internal review
  • Identifying relevant research or prior art
  • Generating test cases or example code for illustration
  • Translating content between languages (with human review)
  • Formatting and restructuring existing content
  • Brainstorming and ideation

Inappropriate Uses

AI tools shall not be used to:

  • Generate final normative content without substantial human review and modification; be aware that content created solely by an AI may not be subject to copyright protection under applicable law
  • Replace human technical judgment or expertise
  • Process Restricted materials in training-enabled systems
  • Circumvent SC's deliberative processes
  • Generate content that will be attributed to a human author without disclosure of AI origin
  • Analyze confidential materials in non-secure AI systems
  • Input personal information about identifiable individuals unless necessary and authorized

Reporting

Any individual who becomes aware of a violation of this policy shall report the matter to the chair of the related working group or to another appropriate representative of SMPTE.

Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, including without limitation revocation of access to SMPTE activities and resources.

Disclaimer:

AI tools do not independently represent SMPTE positions, policies, or official guidance. All 
authoritative decisions, technical conclusions, and normative content originate from SMPTE committees, 
working groups, and human subject matter experts. Users of this document should rely on official SMPTE 
publications and approved standards for definitive information.
